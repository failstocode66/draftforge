## Angle: myth_buster

This source corrects a misconception, fear, or objection. Prioritize:

- `hook` — name the myth or fear plainly so a reader recognizes their own worry
  ("Worried you'll feel trapped?"). The recognizable fear *is* the hook.
- `core_benefit` — the reassurance/payoff once the myth is dismantled (e.g.
  "effortless, fully-in-your-control floating").
- `audience` — the people who hold this fear ("anxious first-timers",
  "claustrophobia-prone"). Myth-busters almost always have a clear audience —
  capture it.
- `claim` / `claim_type` — the *corrective* fact. Type it `hard` only if it's a
  physical fact ("the salt density makes sinking impossible"); otherwise `soft`.
  Capture `supporting_source` if the correction cites one.
- `suggested_cta` — an invitation to try it now that the fear is addressed.

Lead with the objection, not the rebuttal — the fear is what hooks the reader.
